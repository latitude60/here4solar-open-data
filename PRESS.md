# Media and citation note

## One-sentence summary

Here4Solar's dated review of 30 supplier-published flat-rate UK solar export products found a 25p/kWh headline maximum, but only 4.1p/kWh when matching-import, tied-purchase and battery requirements all had to be explicitly absent.

## Checked findings

The version 2.0 dataset was checked against Ofgem and supplier-owned public pages on 10 July 2026. It records:

- all 16 companies on Ofgem's 2026/27 mandatory and voluntary SEG licensee list;
- 30 supplier-published flat-rate records across 17 public brands;
- a 25p/kWh public headline maximum, or 20p/kWh among current Ofgem-listed licensees;
- a 4.1p/kWh maximum when all three tested ties were explicitly absent;
- 15 of 30 rates requiring matching electricity import supply;
- eight of 30 tied to specified equipment or an installation route;
- four of 30 explicitly requiring a battery;
- three licensees for which no current public rate was located on the official supplier site.

Good Energy separately confirmed on 10 July 2026 that 12p/kWh was the standard Solar Savings rate for new applications. Report version 1.1 records that clarification.

## Important limitations

- This is a dated editorial snapshot, not a live tariff feed or recommendation.
- Dynamic and time-of-use tariffs are excluded from the one-number comparison.
- `not_stated` means a condition was not explicit on the checked public summary; it does not prove the full contract has no condition.
- A public-source gap is a discoverability finding, not a conclusion about regulatory compliance.
- Rates and eligibility rules can change, so current supplier terms should be checked before publication or switching.
